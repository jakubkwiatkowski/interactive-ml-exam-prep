1. True risk and empirical hold-out risk

The true risk is the unknown target:

\[R(f)=\mathbb{E}_{(x,y)\sim D}\left[L(y,f_S(x))\right].\]

The hold-out estimator is:

\[R_T(f)=\frac{1}{m'}\sum_{i=1}^{m'}L(y_i,f_S(x_i)).\]

For 0/1 loss:

\[R_T(f)=\frac{\#\text{errors on }T}{m'}=\frac{k}{m'}.\]

2. Bernoulli → Binomial → error rate

\[Z_i=L(y_i,f_S(x_i))\in\{0,1\},\qquad Z_i\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(p),\qquad p=R(f).\]
\[K=\sum_{i=1}^{m'}Z_i,\qquad K\sim\mathrm{Binomial}(m',p),\qquad \hat p=\frac{K}{m'}.\]

3. Three uncertainty paths

Binomial inversion:

\[P(K=k\mid p)=\binom{m'}{k}p^k(1-p)^{m'-k}.\]

Gaussian approximation:

\[CI=\hat p\pm z_{1-\delta/2}\sqrt{\frac{\hat p(1-\hat p)}{m'}}.\]

Hoeffding bound:

\[P\left(|R_T(f)-R(f)|>\epsilon\right)\le 2e^{-2m'\epsilon^2}.\]
\[\epsilon=\sqrt{\frac{1}{2m'}\ln\left(\frac{2}{\delta}\right)}.\]

4. Required test-set size

\[m'\ge \frac{1}{2\epsilon^2}\ln\left(\frac{2}{\delta}\right).\]

Conclusion: \(m'\) is the number of independent test examples needed for a desired error margin \(\epsilon\) and confidence \(1-\delta\).
